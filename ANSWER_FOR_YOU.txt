Ah, you ran into Apple/Google's strict security rules! 

Modern phones completely block the GPS feature unless the website is loaded securely using `https://`. Because our camera was using normal `http://`, the phone immediately blocked it and threw an error, even though you had location turned on.

I just pushed a major update that:
1. Forces the camera to use secure `https://`.
2. Prints out the exact GPS coordinates to the screen when you click the button.
3. Gives you a clickable link that opens directly in Mapy.cz so you can visually verify it!

To install this update on the camera, this is the perfect time to test out our new 1-click auto-installer!
Just paste this into your SSH terminal:

curl -sSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/jakubstenc/YOLO_camera/master/install.sh | bash

HOW TO ACCESS IT NOW:
Because we switched to secure HTTPS, you must now type `https://` in your browser:
https://192.168.97.199:5000

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Your phone browser will show a scary warning saying "This connection is not private". This is completely normal because our offline camera doesn't have an official internet certificate. 
Just click "Advanced" and then "Proceed to 192.168.97.199". 

Once you are in, click the GPS button. It will ask for permission, grab the coordinates, and give you the Mapy.cz link!
